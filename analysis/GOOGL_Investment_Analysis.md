# 1. Investment Memo

## 1. Thesis (one sentence)

Long Alphabet at ~$345: Q1 2026 confirms Google Cloud is converting a $460B backlog at ~27% operating margin while Search continues to grow 19% YoY despite full AI Overviews rollout, and a ~23x forward multiple does not yet price the durability of either trend; sized 4–5% of a balanced book with capacity to add to 6–7% on a 10–15% pullback.

## 2. Why now

Three things changed in the last six months that the consensus model has not fully absorbed.

**First**, Q1 2026 (reported tonight) showed Google Cloud accelerating to **63% YoY revenue growth ($20.03B)**, with backlog roughly doubling sequentially to **$460B**. That backlog is approximately 23x current quarterly Cloud revenue. Cloud operating margin has expanded from ~20% a year ago to **~27%**, with management pointing to continued mix benefits as enterprise AI Solutions becomes the dominant growth driver — a category Pichai called "our primary growth driver for cloud for the first time" on the call.

**Second**, the Gemini market-share narrative has reversed. Per Similarweb, Gemini's share of generative-AI chatbot web traffic moved from ~5.7% in January 2025 to ~21.5% in January 2026, while ChatGPT fell from ~86.7% to ~64.5%. Apptopia mobile data shows Gemini's app share rising from 14.7% to 25.2%, with ChatGPT falling from 69% to 45%. Gemini monthly actives went from ~350M (April 2025) to ~750M (Q4 2025). The "ChatGPT will kill Search" thesis that drove GOOGL to ~17x in March 2025 has been disproved by usage data. The driver is distribution, not model quality — Gemini ships embedded in Android, Chrome, and Workspace.

**Third**, the antitrust tail is shorter and more bounded. Judge Mehta's September 2025 remedies kept Chrome and Android intact and imposed behavioral restrictions only. The DOJ appealed in February 2026 seeking Chrome divestiture, but the D.C. Circuit is not expected to hear arguments until late 2026 / early 2027. The worst-case structural outcome is now a tail event with a clear timeline, not the base case.

## 3. What the market is missing

Two specific underappreciations.

**(a) Cloud margin durability.** The Street is modeling continued Cloud growth and margin expansion, but the slope is wrong. Cloud delivered ~700bps of operating margin expansion in roughly two years. With $460B in RPO and a mix shift toward enterprise AI Solutions (higher gross margin than core GCP), if Cloud grows ~40% in 2026 and continues margin progression toward AWS-like economics (mid-30s operating margin), the implied 2027 segment operating income approaches **$25–30B vs. essentially zero two years ago**. That is a structural earnings step-up that the consensus $378 average price target does not appear to fully reflect.

**(b) Optionality not in the model.** On tonight's call, Alphabet disclosed it will begin selling custom TPU chips to select third-party customers for deployment in their own data centers. This is a credible Nvidia-adjacent revenue stream with high gross margin and is not meaningfully in any sell-side model. Add Waymo — which has commercial revenue but no segment disclosure and an unmarked equity value, with an external funding round increasingly likely — and you have two pieces of optionality not in the bridge.

## 4. Evidence

**Q1 2026 highlights (Alphabet 8-K, April 29, 2026):**
- Total revenue: **$109.9B, +22% YoY** (vs. $107.1B consensus)
- Operating income: $39.7B, +30% YoY (operating margin **36.1%, +200bps**)
- Search and Other: $60.4B, **+19% YoY** (still accelerating despite AI Overviews fears)
- YouTube ads: $9.88B (slight miss vs. $9.97B consensus, +11% YoY)
- Google Cloud: $20.03B, **+63% YoY**
- Cloud backlog (RPO): **$460B, ~doubled QoQ**
- Capex: $35.7B Q1; FY26 guide raised to $175–190B; 2027 expected to "significantly increase"
- Net income: $62.6B (includes a $36.9B equity-securities gain; underlying ~$33.9B)
- Total paid subscriptions: 350M
- Gemini Enterprise paid MAU: +40% QoQ
- Wiz acquisition closed March 11, 2026 ($32B all-cash, now in Cloud segment)

**Behavioral / positioning:**
- GOOGL +21% in April into the print; +65% in calendar 2025 (best Mag 7 performer)
- Hedge fund Mag 7 net exposure ~19% (de-crowded from ~21% peak in mid-2024)
- Wall Street consensus PT ~$378 (limited consensus upside; sentiment improved but not euphoric)
- 52-week range: $146.10 — $349.00

**Valuation:**
- TTM P/E: ~30–32x
- Forward P/E: ~23–24x
- 5-year average P/E: ~25x
- Net cash balance sheet, ~10–11% FCF yield ex-capex normalization

The forward multiple is not screaming cheap. For a business compounding revenue at 22% with operating leverage, an accelerating Cloud segment, and option value not in the model, it is reasonable.

## 5. Catalysts (3–12 months)

1. **Q2 2026 print (late July):** the cleanest test of Cloud sustainability above 50% YoY and continued margin progression. Single most important read.
2. **Brinkema AdTech remedies ruling (expected mid-2026):** behavioral remedies = neutral; structural remedies forcing buy-side/sell-side ad-tech split = multi-billion FCF impact and multiple compression.
3. **Gemini 4 launch (rumored H2 2026):** the consumer competitive arc.
4. **TPU external sales ramp:** first major customer disclosures would crystallize a new revenue line.
5. **Waymo:** external funding mark or partial spin would crystallize unmarked equity value.
6. **Antitrust appeals court briefing (late 2026):** binary risk-on for the Search distribution question.

## 6. Risks / bear case

**1. Capex parabola.** $175–190B in FY26, more in 2027. The combined hyperscaler capex picture has parallels to the 2000-era telco overbuild — an industry that confused infrastructure capex with strategic moat and saw ROIC collapse when demand normalized. If AI workload demand softens before depreciation schedules unwind, Alphabet's free cash flow profile becomes unrecognizable.

**2. Antitrust escalation.** DOJ appeal seeks Chrome divestiture. Morgan Stanley framework suggests 5–8% search traffic erosion translating to $15–25B at-risk revenue. AdTech case has a separate structural-remedy pathway. These are tails, not base cases — but they are unhedgeable in the position.

**3. Search durability.** +19% looks great, but the comp gets harder and AI Overviews has documented click-through compression for organic listings (~60% CTR decline when Overviews appear). If the segment rolls below 10% growth without a clear monetization narrative, the multiple compresses.

**4. Crowded long-duration AI capex trade.** Mag 7 net HF exposure ~19%; a macro shock (rates higher, growth scare) hits this cohort first and hardest. GOOGL is not isolated from regime risk.

**5. YouTube softness.** Q1 was the only segment to miss. Connected TV ad demand has been uneven. Not a thesis-breaker but a watch item.

## 7. What would change my mind

I cut or trim materially if:
- Cloud growth prints below 35% YoY for two consecutive quarters (no acquisition adjustment)
- Search and Other revenue grows below 8% YoY without a credible AI Overviews monetization narrative
- D.C. Circuit orders Chrome divestiture (cut at least half on the news)
- 2027 capex guide implies revenue payback period >6 years on current ROIC math
- Operating margin contracts below 32% on a full-quarter basis
- Insider selling acceleration combined with any of the above

## 8. Bottom line

Long Alphabet at ~$345. Position size 4–5% of a balanced equity book, capacity to scale to 6–7% on a 10–15% pullback. This is a core compounder, not a contrarian bet — the contrarian money was made in March/April 2025 at ~$150–160 when AI-loser narrative peaked.

Asymmetry over 12 months: base case **~$420–450** (24–26x my $17–18 forward EPS, +20–25%), bear case **~$240–260** on combined antitrust escalation and Cloud disappointment (-25–30%). Reward-to-risk approximately 1:1 on a single-name basis. Modest, not screaming.

The reason to own this is regime fit: quality compounders in a regime of sustained AI capex demand, no rate shock, and no structural antitrust action are exactly what should sit in the core of the book. The trade is "Cloud margin curve, plus Search durability, plus capex discipline." Three measurable things, all flagged. None requires believing AI is the future. They require this specific company continuing to execute what it just executed.

# 3. Substack-style Post

## The Easy Money on Alphabet Is Gone. The Trade Isn't.

There's a kind of investment thesis I find boring: the one where the answer is "obvious" and the only question is sizing. Alphabet at $345 is not that thesis. The easy money — the deep contrarian bet at ~$150 in March 2025, when consensus had decided ChatGPT was going to kill Search — is gone. The stock has done +65% since, and another +21% in April alone, into tonight's Q1 2026 print.

What's left is more interesting: a quality compounder where the next leg depends on three discrete things working out, not a single grand narrative. That's the kind of setup I'd rather own than a story stock — but it requires being honest about what's already in the price.

### What changed in 12 months

Twelve months ago, the consensus on Alphabet looked like this: Search is a melting ice cube as ChatGPT eats long-tail queries, Google Cloud is a third-place commodity that will be margin-impaired in perpetuity, and the company is about to torch tens of billions on AI capex with no clear ROI. The multiple compressed to ~17x. Every leg of that has weakened in measurable ways.

On AI usage: Gemini's share of generative-AI chatbot web traffic went from ~5.7% in January 2025 to ~21.5% in January 2026, per Similarweb. ChatGPT's share fell from ~86.7% to ~64.5% over the same window. Apptopia mobile-app data shows an even starker shift — Gemini's app share rose from 14.7% to 25.2%, while ChatGPT fell from 69% to 45%. Gemini monthly actives went from roughly 350 million in April 2025 to 750 million by Q4. This isn't a story about model quality, which has largely converged. It's a distribution story. Gemini ships embedded in Android, Chrome, and Workspace. ChatGPT requires conscious adoption. In hindsight, it shouldn't have surprised anyone that distribution beat capability.

On Cloud: Q1 2026 revenue grew 63% to $20.03 billion. Backlog (RPO) roughly doubled sequentially to $460 billion — more than five years of forward visibility at current quarterly run rate. Operating margin has expanded from roughly 20% a year ago to about 27%. The "third-place commodity" framing doesn't survive that math.

On antitrust: Judge Mehta's September 2025 remedies ruling left Chrome and Android intact and imposed behavioral restrictions only — a meaningful loss for the DOJ. The government appealed in February 2026 seeking Chrome divestiture, but the D.C. Circuit is not expected to hear arguments until late 2026 or early 2027. The worst-case outcome is now a tail with a clear timeline, not the base case.

### What the model probably misses

Even after the rerate, I think the consensus model under-weights two specific things.

First, Cloud margin durability. The Street is modeling continued growth and margin expansion, but the path toward AWS-like economics (mid-30s operating margin) is steeper and faster than the consensus print. Enterprise AI Solutions is now Cloud's primary growth driver, and that mix carries higher gross margin than core GCP. If Cloud comps anywhere near 50% in 2026 with continued margin progression, segment operating income approaches $25–30 billion in 2027 — vs. essentially nothing two years ago. That's an enormous earnings tailwind that the $378 average price target implicitly does not incorporate.

Second, optionality not in the model. On tonight's call, Alphabet disclosed it will begin selling custom TPU chips to select third-party customers for deployment in their own data centers. This is a credible Nvidia-adjacent revenue stream with high gross margins. It's not in any sell-side model meaningfully. Add Waymo — which has commercial revenue, no segment disclosure, and an unmarked equity value — and there are two pieces of optionality that don't show up anywhere in fair-value math.

### Where I'm likely wrong

Discipline requires being specific about how this trade dies.

The clearest risk is capex. Alphabet raised its 2026 capex guide to $175–190 billion and said 2027 will "significantly increase." The combined hyperscaler capex story has echoes of 2000-era telco overbuild — an industry that confused infrastructure investment with strategic moat and saw ROIC collapse when demand normalized. If AI workload demand softens before the depreciation schedule unwinds, Alphabet's free cash flow profile becomes unrecognizable from today's. The forward multiple I'm paying becomes the wrong reference frame.

The second risk is antitrust. Mehta's remedies were the friendly outcome. The DOJ's appeal is the bear's call option. The AdTech case before Judge Brinkema, where remedies are pending, could force a structural split of buy-side and sell-side ad-tech assets — separate from the Search case. Chrome divestiture is the most-cited tail; a Morgan Stanley framework suggests 5–8% search traffic erosion translating to $15–25 billion in at-risk revenue.

The third is search durability. Q1's +19% YoY in Search and Other looks great, but the comp gets harder, and AI Overviews has documented click-through compression for organic results. The bull thesis requires Search to stay above ~10% growth. If it rolls below 8% with no clear AI Overviews monetization story, the whole multiple breaks.

### How I'm sizing it

Long Alphabet at ~$345. Position size 4–5% in a balanced equity book, with capacity to scale to 6–7% on a 10–15% pullback. Core compounder, not max-conviction shot.

I'd cut materially if Cloud growth prints below 35% YoY for two consecutive quarters, if Search rolls below 8% without a clear monetization story, if the D.C. Circuit orders Chrome divestiture, or if 2027 capex guidance implies a revenue payback period beyond six years.

Asymmetry over 12 months: base case ~$420–450 (24–26x my $17–18 forward EPS, +20–25%), bear case ~$240–260 on combined antitrust escalation and Cloud disappointment (-25–30%). The reward-to-risk is roughly 1:1 on a single-name basis. It's not screaming. The reason to own this isn't heroic asymmetry — it's regime fit. Quality compounders in a regime of sustained AI capex demand with no rate shock or structural antitrust action are exactly what you want in the core of the book.

The trade is "Cloud margin curve, plus Search durability, plus capex discipline." Three things, all measurable, all flagged. None of them requires believing AI is the future. They require this specific company continuing to do what it just did.

That's the bet.
